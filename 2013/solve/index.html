2013 Problem Definitions

We’ve done some ground work to collect a set of local problem definitions from local non-profits and municipal organizations.  Keep in mind that there’s the national list of datasets and challenges to pull from as well, but this list is a little closer to home.

Interactive History

The Bangor Museum and History Center has more than 40,000 artifacts in their collection, some of which have been cataloged and digitized.  This dataset of photographs, letters, and information on artifacts lives int he museum’s computer systems, and cannot be accessed except by physically going to the museum.  The museum would love to see an app created which would make it possible for this information to be brought into the community through movable screen-based installations, websites, or even an augmented-reality mobile app.

Event Scheduling, Planning, Listing

So you’ve got an event to plan for a specific target audience, let’s take health care professionals for example.  How do you ensure that your event will not conflict with another event targeting this same audience?  The only way to do it now is by reaching out to your social network, which can be effective, but is not reliable, systematic, or efficient.  Imagine a system in which event organizers are able to see all events for a particular region, and which target audiences they are hoping to attend?  This could simplify the event planning process and lead toward higher quality participation at these events.  Event data of this form could also be used to create a centralized event listing platform which could power a community calendar app as well as feed into existing online event listings.

Intersection Collision Data

The Bangor city council has delivered a data set of all reported automobile collisions at intersections in the Bangor region.  This data on the frequency and severity of crashes could very well be used to save lives.  Build an app that can answer some of the following questions.  Which intersections are the most dangerous?  How can we improve their safety?

Municipal Vehicle Tracking

During a snowstorm, the city can receive hundreds of calls inquiring when a street will be plowed, when it was last plowed, why it hasn’t been plowed yet, why it hasn’t been plowed again, why a street is being plowed at all, why one street seems to be plowed better than another, etc, etc.  There are also occasional claims that a plow hit a mailbox or parked car, which are difficult to dispute without an impartial record of where a truck was at a given time.  Real-time (and historic) vehicle tracking could be valuable for not only plowing, but other functions as well, such as street sweeping or mowing.

The City of Bangor is currently evaluating options for both a hardware GPS tracking unit and a software package for working with the tracking data to visualize and analyze it.  Prototype a hardware or software component of this system to potentially save tax payer dollars by increasing equipment efficiency.

Grant Funded Research Repository

Grant funded research currently operates by allocated a sum of money to a research endeavor.  To make good on the grant’s funding condition, the researching party will need to produce a report and other materials to share the knowledge gained in their investigation.  Currently these data sets and reports created through grant funding are stored in digital format but not in an open system.  Build a content management system geared toward the needs of grant-funded research.

Web Controlled Museum Exhibit

Hardware platforms such as the arduino and raspberryPi have made it affordable and attainable to control real-world physical objects over the internet. Create a model that can be observed and controlled on a smartphone on a computer.  For example, the Maine Discovery Museum has a model of a ship with a  crane which can move cargo between the ship and the dock.  In such an example two motors could be controlled, one to rotate the crane, and another to extend or retract the cable.  A third control could be added to activate or deactivate a mechanism for holding the cargo, such as a magnet or a gripping claw.  An internet controlled exhibit of this kind could become a new model of exhibit design at the museum while promoting a deeper understanding of technical skills in the community.
